James Gurney

“There is no line between fine art and illustration; there is no high or low art; there is only art, and it comes in many forms.” (p. 12)” ― James Gurney, Imaginative Realism: How to Paint What Doesn't Exist

TimeLine:
  • 14 June 1958- Gurney grew up in Palo Alto, California where his interest in illustration and crafting began.
  • 1979- Graduated with a BA in archaeology at the University of California, Berkeley
  • 1983- Worked on projects for National Geographic
  • 1992- Release of Dinotopia: A Land Apart from Time to which received multiple awards, sold over a million copies, and translated into 18 languages.
  • 2012- Became a Living Master by the Art Renewal Center
  • 1989- was awarded Best of Show award from World Sceince Fiction Convention
Color and Light

Gurney is well known for his Dinotopia series and and the stylistic choice of realism from plein air and oil paints. Combining the field of history, physics, and art is one of Gurney's best contribution as this not only fuels the imagination of what but also why and how.
